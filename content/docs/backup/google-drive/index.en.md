Google Drive is a cloud storage service provided by Google, and it can be a convenient way to keep your backups in a place that is both familiar and easy to access. Your backup files stay in your own Google account, which means the data remains under your control.

Because this is a cloud backup service, it can also help when something unexpected happens. If your device is lost, damaged, or local data is removed, you can still restore your data from the backup stored in Google Drive.

# How to sign in

To use Google Drive backup, your device needs to support **GMS** (Google Mobile Services), and you need to be signed in to a Google account on that device first.

After that, you can open the app and sign in to the cloud backup service with the Google account that is already available on your device.
